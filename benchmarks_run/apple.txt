Apple Inc. was founded on April 1, 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne in Cupertino, California. Wayne sold his 10% stake back to Jobs and Wozniak twelve days later for $800. The Apple I personal computer, designed by Steve Wozniak, was the company's first product. The company was incorporated as Apple Computer, Inc. on January 3, 1977, and renamed Apple Inc. on January 9, 2007 when Steve Jobs unveiled the iPhone.

Steve Jobs served as the Chief Executive Officer of Apple Inc. from 1997 until his resignation on August 24, 2011. Tim Cook, who had been Chief Operating Officer since 2007, succeeded Jobs as CEO. Steve Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011, from complications of a pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor. Tim Cook was born on November 1, 1960 in Mobile, Alabama and graduated from Auburn University with a degree in Industrial Engineering.

Apple's headquarters, Apple Park, is located in Cupertino, California, and opened to employees in April 2017. The campus was designed by Norman Foster and Foster + Partners. Apple Park houses approximately 12,000 employees and the building is shaped like a ring, earning it the nickname "the spaceship."
